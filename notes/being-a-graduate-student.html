Being a graduate student

Punctuality

If a meeting’s start time is, say, 9 am. I expect you to be there and ready for the meeting by 8:55 am. I make every effort to get to our meetings on time. I realize that many times I am myself not on time, my lateness; however, doesn’t excuse the fact many of you are unable to make it on time to many of our meetings.

I want to be clear that these meetings should not be mistaken with undergraduate lectures, where we see a number of students sauntering into the lecture room, biting into a sandwich, 20 minutes after the class start time. Such behaviour is rarely tolerated in professional settings. Graduate school is like a job in this regards.

Group meetings

Group meetings are not optional. If for some reason you are unable to make it to a group meeting, please let me know in advance explaining the reasons why you cannot make it to a meeting.

TA duties and Office Hours

TAs are not permitted to host office hours in the lab. This is disruptive to other students and presents a security risk. Please talk to your TA coordinator about booking a consultation room.

Computer Science Seminar Series

Attendance at the weekly seminar series is mandatory for all members (including honours thesis students) unless excused. Watch for announcements.

Lab Environment

If you find the lab too hot / too cold or there are maintenance issues, bring them to my attention immediately and they will contact facilities directly. Similarly, if there are equipment problems (anything from computers to chairs), let the lab coordinator know. These things should not interfere with your ability to do your work. Please, DO NOT attempt to solve these problems for yourself.

Lab Attendance

I expect you to be in the lab for a significant portion of every working day.

  • To create a collegial environment which is lively and energizing. This is something you have asked for. I often hear the suggestion that the way to make the lab a better place is to have more students in there. It doesn’t work if 5-6 students don’t show up on any given day.
  • To ensure your progress. Graduate school is like a job – a tough job with low pay and high expectations! I get that it’s hard and at times you need a break. But, if you maintain some regularity in your working hours, it develops some level of discipline and leads to progress on your projects. Finishing your degree on time (or at all) is contingent on putting in the requisite hours, which, I’m afraid to say, are more than usual full-time hours (40 hours or more!)
  • To improve our working relationship. I can’t help you if I don’t see you around. Also, I will be writing your reference letters and I want to be able to say so-and-so is hard working, reliable, always attends meetings, regularly provides updates and meets milestones, etc. I will not say these things if they are not true.

A Respectful, Friendly and Collegial Environment

Remember that the lab is a shared space. Be considerate of others. Help each other. Try to create an environment that fosters creativity and friendships. I cannot stress enough that you have a unique opportunity to make connections and establish friendships that will last a lifetime.

Food

I will request that you keep food consumption in the lab to a minimum. UOIT has spaces that you can use to have a meal. Water and coffee is okay.

Keeping it Tidy

Please ensure that your desk is clean and tidy. Many a times we have lab visitors. An untidy lab looks unprofessional.

Access

The lab is restricted to students assigned to the room and all CS Faculty. Visitors, of course, are permitted on occasion, but it is expected that if you have visitors, you will keep your conversations to a level that will not disturb the work of others.
